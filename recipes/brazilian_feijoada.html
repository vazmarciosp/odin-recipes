Brazilian Feijoada

Home

Description

Feijoada is a traditional Brazilian stew made with black beans and a variety of pork cuts, slowly cooked until the flavors blend into a rich, hearty dish.

It is typically served with white rice, sautéed collard greens, orange slices, and farofa, making it a complete and comforting meal that is perfect for weekends and family gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 500 g black beans, soaked overnight
  • 300 g pork shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 200 g smoked sausage, sliced
  • 150 g bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Water to cover the beans and meat

Steps

  1. Soak the black beans in plenty of water overnight, then drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, fry the bacon until it releases some fat, then add the sausage and pork shoulder and brown them on all sides.
  3. Add the chopped onion and garlic to the pot and cook until they soften and become fragrant.
  4. Stir in the drained beans, add the bay leaves, and pour in enough water to cover everything by a few centimeters.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let the feijoada simmer gently for 1.5 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally and adding more water if needed.
  6. When the beans are tender and the broth is thick, season with salt and black pepper to taste and simmer for a few more minutes.
  7. Serve hot with white rice, sautéed collard greens, orange slices, and farofa.
