Pão de Queijo

Home

Description

Pão de queijo is a traditional Brazilian cheese bread made with tapioca flour, eggs, milk, and cheese, baked until golden and lightly crisp on the outside.

Soft and chewy on the inside, these small rolls are usually served warm for breakfast or as a snack with coffee, and are loved for their rich, cheesy flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup tapioca flour (tapioca starch)
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup grated cheese (such as mozzarella or Minas cheese)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F) and lightly grease a mini muffin tin or baking tray.
  2. In a small saucepan, heat the milk, vegetable oil, and salt until just steaming, then remove from the heat.
  3. Place the tapioca flour in a bowl and carefully pour the warm liquid over it, stirring until it forms a sticky dough.
  4. Let the mixture cool slightly, then add the egg and mix until smooth before stirring in the grated cheese.
  5. Scoop spoonfuls of the dough into the prepared tin or shape small balls with greased hands and place them on the tray.
  6. Bake for 15 or 20 minutes, or until the pão de queijo is puffed and lightly golden on top, then serve warm.
